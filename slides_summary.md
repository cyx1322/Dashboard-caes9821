# Slides Summary

## Slide 1 — A24 Genre Profitability
- **Horror dominance:** 12.9× average ROI, $98M average franchise profit; franchises deliver 17.41 ROI vs. 5.86 stand-alone.  
- **Timing signals:** 2009 and 2015 horror clusters produced 46.9× and 36.7× ROI; fast production keeps pace with cultural buzz.  
- **Implication:** Prioritize horror sequels/anthologies to reuse marketing momentum and capitalize quickly on trend spikes.

## Slide 2 — Mid-Budget Expansion Plays
- **Romance:** ~$23M budgets yield $91M profit (4.39× ROI).  
- **Musical:** ~$64M budgets return $158M profit (2.57× ROI).  
- **Keywords:** Co-finance with marquee talent, bundle music/awards campaigns, stagger launches with horror slate.  
- **Implication:** Pilot prestige romance or musical events every 1–2 years for diversified upside without blockbuster risk.

## Slide 3 — Audience Geography
- **Domestic-heavy genres:** Comedy (64% US revenue) and Documentary (75% US share); emphasize North American theatrical and streamer deals.  
- **International-reliant genres:** Fantasy (59% overseas) and Animation (58% overseas); need pre-sales, localization, global marketing partners.  
- **Implication:** Match distribution strategy to genre geography to optimize returns.

## Slide 4 — Greenlight Criteria Refresh
- **Reality check:** Critic score vs. ROI correlation ≈ 0.04; acclaim alone doesn’t ensure profit.  
- **Slate actions:** Capture horror playbook lessons, score pitches on franchise potential/budget discipline/audience fit, relax critic-score gatekeeping.  
- **Implication:** Rebalance greenlight decisions toward economic fundamentals and concept strength.
